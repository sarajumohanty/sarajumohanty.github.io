Coure Syllabus:

What the course is about?

This course is about to design digital systems using field-programmable gate arrays (FPGAs) and other field-programmable logic devices (FPLDs). It also includes introduction to electronic design automation (EDA) tools because they have become indispensable to current VLSI designs. The objectives of the course are to provide you a working knowledge and an in-depth understanding of FPGAs and the EDA tools so that you can utilize them effectively in digital design. Students taking this course will also learn basic concept of hardware description language (VHDL) and are expected to work in multiple projects in the laboratory to design and simulate typical digital circuits using Xilinx's ISE software and FPGA board.

What are the design and the goals of this course?

There are a few important components in the course.
  • It aims to provide you an in-depth understanding of the technologies and the architectures of the field-programmable logic devices.
  • It aims to give you the general knowledge of the CAD issues related to circuit implementation on programmable logic devices.
  • The labs are intended to give you the opportunity to gain hands-on experience in using commercial EDA tools for design,
    and to use hardware description language to synthesize hardware design.
The following topics will be covered.
  • Integrated Circuit Design Methodologies
  • Introduction to FPLDs
  • Programming Technologies and Architectures of FPLDs
  • Commercial FPLD Examples
  • Design Flow and Design Tools
  • Hardware Description Languages (VHDL)

Prerequisite for this course:

Knowledge on combinational circuit and sequential circuit design.
